+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| ER Diagram Element            | Heuristics                                          | Definition                                                                                                                                 |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Entity                        | Heuristic 1                                         | A common noun may indicate an entity. For example, "An ​employee​ is working on at least one project."                                       |
|                               | Heuristic 2                                         | Every proper noun should be ignored when identifying entities, like location names or person names.                                        |
|                               | Heuristic 3                                         | When consecutive nouns are present, check the last noun against a predefined set to determine if it's an entity or attribute.              |
|                               | Heuristic 4                                         | Certain nouns such as “record”, “system”, “information”, and “organization” are generally not considered as entities.                      |
|                               | Heuristic 5                                         | Common nouns predominantly used in text scenarios can be identified as entities.                                                           |
|                               | Heuristic 6                                         | The common noun immediately followed by “each” can be an entity.                                                                           |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Attribute                     | Heuristic 7                                         | Nouns that are followed by the phrases “has” or “have” typically represent attribute types.                                                |
|                               | Heuristic 8                                         | Nouns following the phrases “has” or “have” and preceded with a pronoun are attributes belonging to the previously mentioned entity.       |
|                               | Heuristic 9                                         | In text, a sequence of words separated by commas often indicates attributes.                                                               |
|                               | Heuristic 10                                        | When words follow the phrase “of the”, the noun after becomes the parent entity of the listed attributes.                                  |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Primary Key Attribute         | Heuristic 11                                        | The adverb “unique” can indicate the primary key of an entity.                                                                             |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Multi-Valued Attribute        | Heuristic 12                                        | If a noun follows the keyword “several” and isn't an entity, it becomes a multi-valued attribute.                                          |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Composite Attribute           | Heuristic 13                                        | If a noun is followed by a sequence of nouns in brackets, it becomes a composite parent attribute with the bracketed nouns as its children.|
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Relationship                  | Heuristic 14                                        | Relationships can be inferred from verbs placed between two entities.                                                                      |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| One to One Cardinality        | Heuristic 15                                        | Singular nouns often indicate entities in a "one" cardinality relationship.                                                                |
|                               | Heuristic 16                                        | When the verb is in singular form, it may indicate a one to one cardinality relationship.                                                  |
|                               | Heuristic 17                                        | The word “uniquely” indicates “one” cardinality in relationships.                                                                          |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| One to Many Cardinality       | Heuristic 18                                        | Singular nouns paired with certain phrases represent one side of a one-to-many cardinality relationship.                                   |
|                               | Heuristic 19                                        | Phrases like “only one”, “one”, “exactly one”, “uniquely” typically indicate a one cardinality side in a relationship.                     |
|                               | Heuristic 20                                        | Singular nouns paired with specific phrases indicate one side of a one-to-many cardinality relationship.                                   |
|                               | Heuristic 21                                        | If a verb is singular but is followed by phrases like 'number of', it might indicate a one-to-many cardinality relationship.               |
|                               | Heuristic 22                                        | The phrase “no more than” can indicate one cardinality in a relationship.                                                                  |
|                               | Heuristic 23                                        | Plural nouns and certain phrases denote the “many” side of a one-to-many cardinality relationship.                                         |
+-------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Many to Many Cardinality      | Heuristic 24                                        | Sentences describing the cardinality of two entities in sequence suggest a many-to-many relationship.                                      |
|                               | Heuristic 25                                        | Plural nouns and specific phrases in tandem usually indicate both sides of a many-to-many cardinality relationship.                        |
